## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify the names of God in Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion
2. Explain the meanings and significance of different names of God
3. Compare and contrast how God is referred to in Ghana's major religions
4. Discuss how understanding God's names promotes respect and peaceful coexistence
5. Apply the moral lessons from God's names to their own lives and relationships

## Main Content

### Names of God in Christianity
In Christianity, God is known by many names that reflect His attributes and character. Some of the most common names include:
- **Yahweh**: The personal name of God, meaning "I AM WHO I AM" (Exodus 3:14)
- **Elohim**: A plural form used to refer to God's majesty and power (Genesis 1:1)
- **Adonai**: Meaning "Lord" or "Master," emphasizing God's sovereignty (Psalm 8:1)
- **El Shaddai**: "God Almighty," showcasing God's strength and sufficiency (Genesis 17:1)

[IMAGE: A Christian worshipper praying in a church, with light streaming through a stained-glass window depicting a cross]

These names reveal God as the supreme, all-powerful, and loving Creator who desires a personal relationship with His people.

### Names of God in Islam
In Islam, God is known as **Allah**, which simply means "The God" in Arabic. Muslims believe that Allah has 99 beautiful names that describe His attributes, such as:
- **Ar-Rahman**: The Most Gracious, emphasizing Allah's mercy and compassion
- **Al-Khaliq**: The Creator, acknowledging Allah as the One who brought everything into existence
- **Al-Malik**: The King or Sovereign, recognizing Allah's rule over the universe
- **As-Salam**: The Source of Peace, highlighting Allah's ability to grant peace and security

[IMAGE: Muslim faithful praying in a mosque, with intricate Arabic calligraphy displaying the names of Allah]

By reflecting on these names, Muslims seek to understand and appreciate Allah's greatness and strive to embody His attributes in their own lives.

### Names of God in Traditional African Religion
In Traditional African Religion, God is often referred to as the Supreme Being or Creator, with names varying among different ethnic groups. Some examples include:
- **Nyame** (Akan): The Supreme Being who created all things
- **Mawu** (Ewe): The Creator God who is both male and female
- **Olorun** (Yoruba): The Owner of the Sky, emphasizing God's transcendence
- **Chukwu** (Igbo): The Great God, highlighting God's supremacy

These names reflect the African understanding of God as the ultimate source of life, wisdom, and moral guidance.

| Religion | Example Names of God |
|----------|----------------------|
| Christianity | Yahweh, Elohim, Adonai, El Shaddai |
| Islam | Allah, Ar-Rahman, Al-Khaliq, Al-Malik, As-Salam |
| Traditional African Religion | Nyame, Mawu, Olorun, Chukwu |

## Examples
1. Abena, a Christian student, faces a difficult decision at school. She remembers that God is called "El Shaddai," meaning "God Almighty," and finds strength in knowing that He will guide and support her in making the right choice.

2. Kwame, a Muslim student, gets into an argument with a classmate. He pauses and reflects on Allah's name "As-Salam," which means "The Source of Peace." Inspired by this, he apologizes to his classmate and works to resolve the conflict peacefully.

3. Kofi, who follows Traditional African Religion, is struggling with a moral dilemma. He recalls the Akan name for God, "Nyame," and seeks wisdom from the Supreme Being to navigate the situation with integrity and compassion.

## Summary
- God is known by various names in Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion, each reflecting unique attributes and characteristics.
- In Christianity, names like Yahweh, Elohim, Adonai, and El Shaddai emphasize God's personal nature, power, sovereignty, and sufficiency.
- Muslims refer to God as Allah and recognize His 99 beautiful names, such as Ar-Rahman (The Most Gracious), Al-Khaliq (The Creator), Al-Malik (The King), and As-Salam (The Source of Peace).
- Traditional African Religion acknowledges God as the Supreme Being or Creator, with names like Nyame (Akan), Mawu (Ewe), Olorun (Yoruba), and Chukwu (Igbo).
- Understanding and respecting the names of God in different religions promotes tolerance, unity, and peaceful coexistence in Ghana's diverse religious landscape.
- By reflecting on the moral lessons embedded in God's names, students can apply these values to their own lives and relationships.
