## Main Content
### Major Physical Features
Ghana's physical landscape is characterized by a variety of features, including:
1. Coastal Plains
2. Forest Belt
3. Savannah
4. Volta Basin

[IMAGE: A colorful map of Ghana showing the different physical regions]

### Landscape and Water Bodies
Ghana's landscape is diverse, with each region having its own unique characteristics:

- The **Coastal Plains** stretch along the Gulf of Guinea and feature sandy beaches, lagoons, and low-lying areas.
- The **Forest Belt**, which includes the Ashanti region, is known for its dense tropical rainforests and rich biodiversity.
- The **Savannah**, covering the northern part of Ghana, is characterized by grasslands, scattered trees, and a dry climate.
- The **Volta Basin**, dominated by the Volta River and Lake Volta, is a major source of water for irrigation, transportation, and hydroelectric power.

### Physical Features and Human Activities
Ghana's physical features have a significant impact on human activities and settlements:

| Physical Feature | Human Activities |
|------------------|------------------|
| Coastal Plains   | Fishing, salt production, tourism |
| Forest Belt      | Farming, logging, mining |
| Savannah         | Animal grazing, agriculture |
| Volta Basin      | Fishing, irrigation, transportation |

### Conserving Ghana's Physical Environment
It is crucial to conserve and protect Ghana's physical environment for sustainable development. Some ways to achieve this include:
- Promoting eco-friendly practices in agriculture, mining, and logging
- Encouraging reforestation and afforestation programs
- Protecting water bodies from pollution and unsustainable fishing practices
- Raising awareness about the importance of environmental conservation

## Examples
1. The Kakum National Park in the Central Region is a prime example of how Ghana's physical features can be conserved while promoting eco-tourism. The park's dense rainforest and diverse wildlife attract visitors from around the world, contributing to the local economy.

2. The Volta River Authority's management of the Akosombo Dam demonstrates how physical features can be harnessed for human benefit. The dam generates hydroelectric power, provides irrigation for farming, and facilitates transportation along the Volta River.

## Summary
- Ghana's major physical features include the Coastal Plains, Forest Belt, Savannah, and Volta Basin.
- Each region has its unique landscape and water bodies, influencing human activities and settlements.
- Physical features play a crucial role in shaping Ghana's economy, with activities such as fishing, farming, mining, and tourism.
- Conserving Ghana's physical environment is essential for sustainable development.
- Examples like the Kakum National Park and Akosombo Dam showcase the importance of managing physical features effectively.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Physical Features | Natural characteristics of the Earth's surface, such as mountains, rivers, and plains |
| Coastal Plains | Low-lying areas along the coast, often characterized by sandy beaches and lagoons |
| Forest Belt | A region dominated by dense tropical rainforests, found in southern Ghana |
| Savannah | Grasslands with scattered trees, typically found in northern Ghana |
| Volta Basin | The area drained by the Volta River and its tributaries, including Lake Volta |
| Biodiversity | The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat or ecosystem |
| Irrigation | The application of controlled amounts of water to plants at needed intervals |
| Hydroelectric Power | Electricity generated by the energy of falling or flowing water |
| Reforestation | The process of planting trees in an area where they have been previously cut down |
| Sustainable Development | Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs |
