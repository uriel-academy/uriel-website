## Introduction
Home security is a critical life skill for every Ghanaian student to master. Whether you live in a busy city like Accra or Kumasi, or a small village, keeping your home and family safe is essential. Many Ghanaian entrepreneurs like locksmiths, security system installers, and cybersecurity experts have built successful careers around helping people protect their homes. By learning these skills now, you'll be better prepared to secure your own home in the future, and maybe even start a business providing these valuable services to your community.

Imagine coming home from the market to discover your house has been broken into and your belongings stolen. Or getting a suspicious email asking for your personal information. These scary situations happen every day in Ghana, but with the right knowledge and preparation, you can greatly reduce your risk. In this lesson, we'll cover practical steps you can take to keep your home safe from both physical and digital threats. 

## Main Content

### Securing Doors and Windows
The first line of defense for any home is strong, secure doors and windows. In Ghana, many homes use **burglar-proof** metal doors and window grills to deter intruders. But even the strongest door is useless if not locked properly. Always install high-quality **padlocks** or **deadbolts** on all exterior doors. 

For windows, in addition to grills, consider installing **window locks** that limit how far they can open. Make sure locks are anchored securely into the window frame.

### Alarms and Cameras
For an extra layer of security, many Ghanaian homes now have **alarm systems**. These can range from simple door and window sensors that trigger a loud siren, to more advanced systems with motion detectors and cameras.  

When choosing an alarm system, look for:
- Reliable sensors for all entry points 
- Backup battery in case of power outages
- Option to alert security company or police
- Cameras to identify intruders

Work with a reputable security company to design and install a system that fits your needs and budget.

### Family Emergency Plan
In addition to physical barriers, every Ghanaian family should have an **emergency plan**. This covers what to do not just during a break-in, but also fires, medical emergencies, and natural disasters. 

Key elements of a family emergency plan:
1. Agree on a safe meeting spot outside the home
2. Memorize important phone numbers (parents, police, fire dept)
3. Know how to exit the home safely from each room
4. Practice safety drills regularly

| Emergency | Key Actions |
|-----------|-------------|
| Break-in | Get to safe room, call police | 
| Fire | Evacuate immediately, meet at safe spot |
| Medical | Call ambulance, provide first aid |
| Earthquake | Take cover, evacuate after shaking stops |

### Online Security 
Home security doesn't end with physical protection. In today's digital world, online threats like **phishing** emails and **malware** can put your personal information at risk. 

Follow these tips to stay safe online:
- Never click links or download attachments from unknown senders
- Don't share sensitive info like passwords or bank details online
- Keep antivirus software updated on all devices
- Teach family about identifying phishing attempts 

Some Ghanaian homes also use **WiFi locks** and **smart security** systems that connect to your phone. While convenient, ensure these have strong passwords and encryption to prevent hacking.

## Practical Activity
Let's practice some basic home security skills:

### Installing a Padlock
Materials:
- Door or gate with hasp 
- Quality padlock with keys

Instructions:
1. Examine padlock to understand how it works
2. Open hasp on door and insert padlock
3. Close hasp and padlock, confirm it's secure
4. Double check you have keys before locking!

[IMAGE: step by step of installing padlock on a typical Ghanaian door]

### Family Fire Drill
Materials: 
- Home layout diagram
- Stopwatch or timer

Instructions:
1. Draw a floor plan of your home, marking all exits
2. Agree on an outdoor meeting spot a safe distance from home
3. Practice two drills: 
- Evacuate home and meet at safe spot, timing how long it takes
- Evacuate at night to practice in low visibility
4. Discuss what went well and areas to improve

Expected outcome: 
Family can safely evacuate home in under 3 minutes day or night and account for all members at meeting spot.

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Burglar-proof | Designed to prevent intruders from entering |
| Padlock | Portable lock with U-shaped shackle that hooks into a hasp |  
| Deadbolt | Heavy duty lock bolt that extends deep into door frame |
| Window lock | Lock that limits how far a window can open |
| Alarm system | Electronic system that detects intruders and sounds alert |
| Backup battery | Battery that powers alarm during electricity outages |
| Emergency plan | Agreed procedures for family to follow in emergency situations |
| Safety drill | Practice of emergency plan to prepare for real scenarios |
| Phishing | Fraudulent attempt to get sensitive information through fake emails or websites |
| Malware | Malicious software designed to damage or gain access to computer systems |
| WiFi lock | Lock that uses wireless signal to activate, often controlled by smartphone |  
| Smart security | Security system with internet-connected devices for remote monitoring and control |
