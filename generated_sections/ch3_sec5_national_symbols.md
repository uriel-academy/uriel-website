## Main Content
### The National Flag of Ghana
The National Flag of Ghana was designed by Theodosia Okoh and adopted on March 6, 1957, when Ghana gained independence from British colonial rule. The flag consists of four colors: red, gold, green, and a black star in the center.

[IMAGE: The National Flag of Ghana, displaying the red, gold, and green horizontal stripes with a black star in the center]

The colors and symbols of the flag have the following meanings:
- **Red**: Represents the blood shed by Ghanaians in their struggle for independence
- **Gold**: Symbolizes the mineral wealth of Ghana
- **Green**: Represents the lush forests and natural resources of the country
- **Black Star**: Signifies African emancipation and unity

### The Coat of Arms of Ghana
The Coat of Arms of Ghana was designed by Amon Kotei and adopted in 1957. It features a shield divided into four quarters by a green St. George's Cross, with a golden lion in the center.

[IMAGE: The Coat of Arms of Ghana, showcasing the shield, golden lion, and the motto "Freedom and Justice"]

The elements of the Coat of Arms symbolize:
1. **Shield**: Represents the traditional warrior's shield, symbolizing defense and protection
2. **Golden Lion**: Signifies the continued link between Ghana and the Commonwealth of Nations
3. **Motto**: "Freedom and Justice" is written below the shield, emphasizing Ghana's commitment to these values

### The National Anthem of Ghana
The National Anthem of Ghana, titled "God Bless Our Homeland Ghana," was composed by Philip Gbeho and adopted in 1957. The anthem is sung at official events, schools, and public gatherings to express national pride and unity.

| Verse | Lyrics |
|-------|--------|
| 1     | God bless our homeland Ghana<br>And make our nation great and strong,<br>Bold to defend forever<br>The cause of Freedom and of Right;<br>Fill our hearts with true humility,<br>Make us cherish fearless honesty,<br>And help us to resist oppressors' rule<br>With all our will and might evermore. |
| 2     | Hail to thy name, O Ghana,<br>To thee we make our solemn vow:<br>Steadfast to build together<br>A nation strong in Unity;<br>With our gifts of mind and strength of arm,<br>Whether night or day, in the midst of storm,<br>In every need, whate'er the call may be,<br>To serve thee, Ghana, now and evermore. |

The anthem encourages Ghanaians to:
- Be bold in defending freedom and justice
- Cherish honesty and humility
- Resist oppression
- Build a strong, united nation
