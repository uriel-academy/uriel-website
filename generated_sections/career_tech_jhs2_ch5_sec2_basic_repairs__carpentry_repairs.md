## Learning Objectives
After completing this section, students will be able to:
1. Identify common carpentry tools and their uses
2. Repair simple wooden furniture like chairs, stools, and tables
3. Replace broken parts of wooden doors and windows
4. Apply appropriate finishes to repaired wooden items

## Introduction
Carpentry is a vital skill in Ghana, with applications in homes, schools, offices, and businesses across the country. From the bustling wood markets of Kumasi to the skilled carpenters crafting kente looms in the Volta Region, working with wood is an essential part of Ghanaian life and culture. As a JHS student, learning basic carpentry repairs will not only help you maintain items in your own home but also open up opportunities for future careers or entrepreneurship. Imagine being able to repair a broken chair for your grandmother or helping your uncle fix a door in his carpentry shop! With the skills from this lesson, you'll be well on your way to becoming a valuable contributor to your community.

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|

| Screwdriver | Driving and removing screws |
| Handsaw | Cutting wood to size |
| Chisel | Shaping and carving wood |
| Measuring Tape | Measuring lengths and marking cuts |
| Sandpaper | Smoothing wood surfaces |

### Repairing Wooden Furniture
1. Assess the damage and determine what repairs are needed (e.g., replacing a broken leg, reinforcing a joint)
2. Gather necessary tools and materials (replacement wood, glue, nails or screws)
3. Remove damaged parts carefully using a hammer, chisel, or saw
4. Cut replacement pieces to size using a handsaw and measuring tape
5. Attach replacement parts securely with glue, nails, or screws
6. Sand repaired areas smooth and apply finish if desired

[IMAGE: Step-by-step process of repairing a broken chair leg, from removal to replacement and finishing]

### Fixing Wooden Doors and Windows
1. Identify the problem (e.g., loose hinges, broken panels, stuck drawers)
2. Remove the door or window from its frame if necessary
3. Replace or tighten loose hinges using a screwdriver
4. Repair or replace broken panels or slats using wood glue and clamps
5. Sand and plane edges of drawers or doors to ensure smooth operation
6. Reinstall the repaired door or window and test for proper function

[IMAGE: Demonstration of tightening loose door hinges with a screwdriver]

### Finishing Repaired Wood Items
After making repairs, protect and enhance the wood with an appropriate finish:

| Finish Type | Application | Advantages |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Paint | Brush or spray | Wide color selection, covers imperfections |
| Stain | Brush, rag, or spray | Enhances wood grain, available in many shades |
| Varnish | Brush | Durable clear coat, protects against moisture |
| Oil | Rag | Easy to apply, natural look and feel |

Remember to work in a well-ventilated area and follow the manufacturer's instructions when applying finishes.

## Practical Activity
### Repairing a Wooden Stool
Materials:
- Damaged wooden stool
- Hammer
- Nails
- Wood glue
- Sandpaper
- Replacement wood (if needed)

Instructions:
1. Examine the stool and identify the areas needing repair (e.g., loose legs, broken seat)
2. If necessary, remove damaged parts using a hammer or chisel
3. Cut replacement pieces from scrap wood using a handsaw and measuring tape
4. Secure loose joints with wood glue and clamps, allowing glue to dry completely
5. Reattach any removed parts using nails and a hammer
6. Sand the repaired areas and the entire stool for a smooth finish
7. Apply a coat of oil or varnish to protect the wood, if desired

Expected Outcome:
A sturdy, functional wooden stool with repairs that blend seamlessly with the original structure.

## Career Connections
Developing carpentry repair skills can lead to various career paths in Ghana:
- Furniture Maker: Create and repair custom furniture for homes and businesses
- Construction Worker: Build and maintain wooden structures in residential and commercial projects
- Antique Restorer: Specialize in repairing and refinishing valuable wooden antiques and heirlooms
- Carpenter's Assistant: Work alongside skilled carpenters to learn the trade and grow your own business
- Entrepreneur: Start a small business offering carpentry repair services to your local community

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Carpentry | The skill of making and repairing wooden objects and structures |
| Chisel | A tool with a sharp, flat blade for shaping and carving wood |
| Grain | The pattern of fibers in wood, which affects its appearance and strength |
| Joint | The point at which two pieces of wood are joined together |
| Mortise | A cavity cut into a piece of wood to receive a tenon |
| Plane | A tool for smoothing and shaping wood by shaving off thin layers |
| Sanding | The process of smoothing wood using abrasive materials like sandpaper |
| Tenon | A projection on the end of a piece of wood, inserted into a mortise to form a joint |
| Veneer | A thin layer of wood applied to the surface of a cheaper material for improved appearance |
| Warping | The distortion of wood caused by changes in moisture content or temperature |
