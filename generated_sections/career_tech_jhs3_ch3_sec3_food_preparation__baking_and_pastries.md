### Preparing the Dough
**Dough** is a mixture of flour, water and other ingredients that forms the base of baked goods. Follow these steps to prepare dough:

1. Wash your hands with soap and clean all surfaces and tools. 
2. Measure the dry ingredients (flour, sugar, baking powder, salt) into a large bowl.
3. Cut cold margarine into small pieces and rub into flour until it resembles breadcrumbs. 
4. Gradually add liquids (water, milk, eggs) and mix until the dough comes together.
5. Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth and elastic.
6. Wrap dough and chill in refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before using.

### Shaping and Frying Bofrot
**Bofrot** or Puff-Puff are sweet, fried dough balls enjoyed as a snack or dessert. To make bofrot:

1. Combine flour, sugar, yeast, and warm water in a bowl. Mix into a soft, sticky dough. 
2. Cover the dough and let rise in a warm place for about 1 hour until doubled in size.
3. Heat oil in a deep pan until hot but not smoking. 
4. Scoop spoonfuls of dough and drop carefully into the oil. Fry until golden brown.
5. Remove bofrot with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels. 
6. Roll in sugar and serve warm.

### Baking Meat Pies and Rock Buns
**Meat pies** are a popular Ghanaian snack with a savory filling in a flaky pastry. **Rock buns** are sweet rolls studded with raisins or currants. Here's how to bake them:

1. Preheat the oven or coal pot. Grease and flour baking sheets.
2. For meat pies: 
   - Roll out pastry dough and cut into circles. 
   - Place a spoonful of cooked meat filling in the center of each circle.
   - Brush edges with egg wash, fold into half-moons, and crimp edges with a fork.
3. For rock buns:
   - Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and spices in a bowl. 
   - Rub in margarine until crumbly. Stir in dried fruits.
   - Add beaten egg and milk. Mix into a stiff dough.
   - Drop spoonfuls of dough onto baking sheets. 
4. Bake meat pies and rock buns in the preheated oven/coal pot until golden brown, about 20-30 minutes. 
5. Remove from oven and let cool slightly before serving.

[IMAGE: Photos of unbaked and baked meat pies and rock buns]

Follow these **food safety rules** when baking:

- Wash your hands before handling food 
- Keep ingredients refrigerated until ready to use
- Avoid touching your face, hair, or phone while baking
- Cook fillings thoroughly to safe temperatures 
- Don't eat raw dough or batter
- Store baked goods properly in airtight containers

## Practical Activity
Now it's your turn to practice baking! Try this activity:

### Rock Bun Bonanza
Work in small groups to bake a batch of rock buns and share with your class.

Materials:
- 200g flour 
- 100g sugar
- 50g margarine
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp each of nutmeg, cinnamon, and salt
- 50g currants or raisins
- 1 egg
- 2 tbsp milk

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 180Â°C and grease baking sheets.
2. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and spices in a bowl. 
3. Rub in margarine with fingertips until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
4. Stir in dried fruits.
5. Beat egg with milk and stir into the dry ingredients to make a stiff dough.
6. Spoon 12 mounds of the mixture onto baking sheets. 
7. Bake for 15-20 minutes until lightly browned. Cool on a wire rack.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Dough | Mixture of flour, liquid and other ingredients that forms the base of baked goods |
| Knead | To work dough by pressing, folding and stretching to make it smooth and elastic | 
| Yeast | Microorganism that makes dough rise by producing carbon dioxide gas |
| Proofing | Letting dough rise in a warm place so the yeast can produce gas |
| Rubbing in | Mixing fat into flour with fingertips until it resembles fine breadcrumbs |
| Fold | To gently combine ingredients by "folding" one part over another |
| Crimp | To seal and decorate the edges of a pie crust by pinching with fingers or a fork |
| Aerate | To add air to a mixture by sifting, whisking, or creaming |
| Curdle | When a liquid mixture separates into lumps due to overheating or adding acid |
| Blind bake | To pre-bake an empty pie shell so it doesn't get soggy when filled |
| Caramelize | Browning of sugar when it is heated, which adds flavor and color | 
| Shortening | A solid fat used in baking to make pastries crumbly or "short" |
