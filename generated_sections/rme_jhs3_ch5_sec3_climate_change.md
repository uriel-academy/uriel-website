## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain the causes and effects of climate change
2. Identify how climate change impacts Ghana and the world
3. Discuss the moral and religious reasons to care for the environment 
4. Describe actions individuals and communities can take to address climate change
5. Connect teachings from Christianity, Islam and Traditional African Religion to environmental stewardship

## Introduction
Kofi, Ama, and their friends live in the bustling city of Accra. They love visiting the beaches, hiking in the lush forests, and enjoying the natural beauty of Ghana. But lately, they've noticed changes – the weather seems hotter and drier, and the rainy season is less predictable. In the mosque, church, and shrine, their religious leaders are talking about a global challenge: climate change.

As students at a diverse JHS in Ghana's capital, Kofi and his classmates are learning about this critical issue. They're discovering how their faith traditions call them to be good stewards of the environment, and how they can make a difference in their communities. Join them on this journey to understand climate change and our moral responsibility to address it.

## Main Content
### What is Climate Change? 
**Climate change** refers to the long-term shifts in global or regional climate patterns. In recent decades, the Earth's average temperature has been increasing, largely due to human activities that release **greenhouse gases** like carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat, causing the planet to warm. The effects of climate change include rising sea levels, more extreme weather events, and changes in precipitation patterns.

### Impacts of Climate Change in Ghana and Worldwide
Ghana is already experiencing the impacts of climate change:
- Longer, more intense dry seasons and shorter, less predictable rainy seasons
- Rising sea levels eroding coastal areas, threatening cities like Accra and Cape Coast
- More frequent and severe floods and droughts, damaging crops and infrastructure

Around the world, climate change is affecting people and ecosystems:
- Melting glaciers and polar ice caps, causing sea level rise
- Shifting habitats and migration patterns for plants and animals
- Increased risk of wildfires, hurricanes, and other extreme weather events

### Religious and Moral Reasons to Care for the Environment
Ghana's major faith traditions – Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion – all teach the importance of caring for the environment:

| Religion | Teaching on Environmental Stewardship |
|----------|---------------------------------------|
| Christianity | In Genesis, God creates the Earth and entrusts humans with its care. As stewards of creation, Christians have a responsibility to protect the environment. |
| Islam | The Quran teaches that Allah created the Earth in balance, and humans should maintain this balance. Muslims are called to avoid waste and excess, and to care for all living things. |
| Traditional African Religion | Many traditional beliefs emphasize the interconnectedness of all life and the sacredness of nature. Humans are part of the natural world and must live in harmony with it. |

Protecting the environment is a moral issue. It's about preserving the Earth for future generations, caring for the most vulnerable people who are impacted by climate change, and respecting the intrinsic value of all creation.

### Taking Action on Climate Change
Individuals and communities in Ghana can take action to address climate change:
1. Conserve energy by turning off lights and unplugging devices when not in use.
2. Reduce waste by recycling, composting, and avoiding single-use plastics.
3. Plant trees and protect green spaces, which absorb carbon dioxide and provide habitats.
4. Support renewable energy projects, like solar and wind power.
5. Educate others about climate change and the importance of environmental stewardship.

Religious communities can also play a role, by:
- Incorporating environmental themes into sermons, prayers, and teachings
- Organizing eco-friendly projects, like tree planting or clean-up days
- Advocating for policies that address climate change and protect the environment

## Examples
1. Kofi's church youth group organizes a campaign to reduce plastic waste in their community. They distribute reusable bags and educate people about the impacts of plastic pollution on the environment and human health. As Christians, they believe it's their duty to care for the Earth and all its inhabitants.

2. Ama's mosque starts a community garden, where members can grow their own vegetables using sustainable practices like composting and water conservation. They share the produce with local families in need, putting into practice the Islamic principles of avoiding waste and caring for the poor.

3. Kwame's traditional religious community holds a ceremony to honor the sacred forest near their village. They teach the younger generation about the importance of preserving this natural area, which provides clean air, water, and habitats for diverse species. They see protecting the forest as a way to maintain balance and harmony with the natural world.

## Summary
- Climate change is a long-term shift in climate patterns, largely caused by human activities that release greenhouse gases.
- Ghana and the world are already experiencing the impacts of climate change, including rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and changes in precipitation.
- Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion all teach the importance of caring for the environment and being good stewards of the Earth.
- Protecting the environment is a moral issue that involves preserving the planet for future generations, caring for vulnerable populations, and respecting the intrinsic value of nature.
- Individuals and communities in Ghana can take action on climate change by conserving energy, reducing waste, planting trees, supporting renewable energy, and educating others.

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Climate change | A long-term shift in global or regional climate patterns |
| Greenhouse gases | Gases in the atmosphere that trap heat, such as carbon dioxide |
| Atmosphere | The layer of gases surrounding the Earth |
| Ecosystem | A community of living organisms interacting with their environment |
| Stewardship | The responsible management and protection of something entrusted to one's care |
| Renewable energy | Energy from sources that are naturally replenished, like sun, wind, and water |
| Sustainability | Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs |
| Biodiversity | The variety of life on Earth, including diversity within and between species |
| Conservation | The protection and preservation of the natural environment and resources |
| Carbon footprint | The amount of greenhouse gases an individual or organization emits |
