## Main Content
### Tools for Taking Measurements
To take accurate body measurements, you will need the following tools:

| Tool | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Tape Measure | A flexible ruler used to measure body circumference and length |
| Measurement Chart | A table to record measurements systematically |
| Pencil or Pen | For recording measurements on the chart |

[IMAGE: Tape measure, measurement chart, and pencil]

### Key Body Measurements
When taking body measurements, it is important to know the key areas to measure. These include:

1. **Bust**: Measure around the fullest part of the chest
2. **Waist**: Measure around the narrowest part of the torso
3. **Hips**: Measure around the fullest part of the hips
4. **Shoulder to Waist**: Measure from the shoulder seam to the waistline
5. **Arm Length**: Measure from the shoulder seam to the wrist

[IMAGE: Diagram showing key body measurements]

### Taking Accurate Measurements
To ensure accurate measurements, follow these steps:

1. Have the person being measured wear close-fitting clothing
2. Make sure the person stands straight with arms relaxed at their sides
3. Hold the tape measure snugly but not too tightly against the body
4. Record measurements in the appropriate column of the measurement chart
5. Double-check each measurement to ensure accuracy

## Practical Activity
1. Divide students into pairs
2. Provide each pair with a tape measure, measurement chart, and pencil
3. Have students take turns measuring each other using the key body measurements
4. Record measurements in the measurement chart
5. Compare measurements with other pairs to check for accuracy
6. Discuss the importance of precise measurements in garment construction

Materials needed:
- Tape measures
- Measurement charts
- Pencils

Expected outcome: Students will have a completed measurement chart with accurate body measurements.
