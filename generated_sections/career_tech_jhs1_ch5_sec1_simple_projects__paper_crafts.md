## Main Content

### Types of Paper and Their Uses
To create successful paper crafts, it's important to choose the right type of paper for your project. Some common types include:
- **Cardstock**: Thick, sturdy paper ideal for making cards, signs, and 3D crafts
- **Construction paper**: Colored paper available in various weights, good for decorations and kids' crafts 
- **Origami paper**: Thin, square paper designed for folding techniques
- **Kraft paper**: Strong brown paper often used for packaging or rustic-look crafts
- **Crepe paper**: Crinkled, stretchy paper in bright colors, perfect for party decorations

### Measuring, Cutting, and Folding Techniques
Precise measuring, cutting, and folding are essential for creating neat, professional-looking paper crafts. 
1. Always use a ruler or measuring tape to mark your measurements before cutting.
2. Use sharp scissors or a craft knife for clean, straight cuts. 
3. For folding, align edges carefully and crease firmly with a bone folder or ruler edge. Practice basic folds like mountain, valley, and accordion folds. 

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Ruler or measuring tape | Accurately measure paper |
| Pencil | Mark measurements and guidelines | 
| Scissors | Cut paper to size and shape |
| Craft knife | Make precise cuts, best for thicker paper |
| Bone folder | Create sharp, clean folds |

### Paper Crafting Techniques

#### Quilling
Quilling involves rolling thin strips of paper into coils, then pinching and shaping them into designs. It's often used to create delicate decorative elements like flowers and patterns.

[IMAGE: Diagram or photo showing quilling techniques and designs]

#### Origami
Origami is the art of folding square paper into 3D shapes and figures, without using glue or scissors. By learning a few basic folds, you can create an endless variety of designs.

Follow these steps to fold a simple origami heart:
1. Start with a square piece of origami paper, colored side down. 
2. Fold the paper in half vertically, crease, then unfold.
3. Fold the top corners down to the center crease.
4. Fold the point at the top of the heart down, about 1/3 of the way.
5. Fold the sides in towards the center.
6. Complete your heart by folding the bottom point up.

#### Decoupage
Decoupage is a technique of decorating objects by gluing paper cutouts onto them, then sealing with varnish. It can be used to personalize items like boxes, frames, or furniture.

Materials needed:
- Paper cutouts (can use wrapping paper, magazine pages, tissue paper)
- Scissors
- Decoupage glue or Mod Podge
- Foam brush
- Varnish or sealer

## Practical Activity
Let's apply our paper crafting skills to create a product commonly sold by Ghanaian businesses - a customized gift bag. You'll use measuring, cutting, folding, and decoupage techniques.

Materials:
- 2 sheets of 12x18 inch cardstock or kraft paper 
- Scissors
- Ruler
- Pencil
- Decorative paper like kente or adinkra-patterned wrapping paper
- Decoupage glue
- Foam brush

Instructions:
1. Measure and cut your cardstock into a 12x12 inch square. 
2. Fold the square in half diagonally to make a triangle, then unfold. 
3. Fold each corner into the center point.
4. Lift the flaps created by the previous folds, creasing only at the bottom. This forms the bag shape.
5. Cut decorative paper into strips and shapes. Glue onto bag using decoupage technique - apply glue to bag, stick on paper, then seal with another layer of glue. Let dry.
6. Fill your bag with tissue paper or a small gift to complete the look.
