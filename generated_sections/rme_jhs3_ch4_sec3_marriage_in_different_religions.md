## Introduction

## Main Content
### Shared Religious Values in Marriage
While there are differences in specific marriage practices between religions, Christianity, Islam and Traditional African Religion share many common values:

- Marriage is a sacred covenant before God/the gods
- Spouses should love, respect and be faithful to each other  
- Marriage is the proper foundation for family life
- Husbands and wives have roles and responsibilities to fulfill

These shared moral foundations help create stable marriages and families, which are the bedrock of a strong society. [IMAGE: A Christian, Muslim, and traditional wedding ceremony, showing shared joy and celebration]

### Christianity and Marriage
For Christians, marriage is a sacred, lifelong union between a man and woman. Key biblical teachings include:

- "Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh." (Genesis 2:24)
- Husbands should "love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her" (Ephesians 5:25) 
- Wives should "submit to your own husbands, as to the Lord." (Ephesians 5:22)
- Divorce is only permitted in cases of adultery or if an unbelieving spouse deserts the marriage

Most Ghanaian churches teach these values of sacrificial love, faithfulness, and clearly defined marital roles as the foundation for strong Christian families. [IMAGE: A couple exchanging rings in a church wedding]

### Islam and Marriage
In Islam, marriage is considered completing "half of one's faith." The Quran and Hadith outline the purpose and responsibilities in Muslim marriages:

- "And of His signs is that He created for you from yourselves mates that you may find tranquility in them; and He placed between you affection and mercy." (Quran 30:21)
- The husband must provide for the family financially and treat his wife with kindness 
- The wife should protect her husband's honor and property, raise children with Islamic values
- The marriage contract (nikah) includes a dowry (mahr) paid from the husband to the wife
- Divorce is allowed but disliked; couples should first seek mediation and reconciliation  

These teachings form the basis for marriage in Ghana's Muslim community, such as the large weddings you may see at your local mosque. [IMAGE: A Muslim couple sitting together after their nikah ceremony]

### Traditional African Religion and Marriage
Traditional Ghanaian religions also view marriage as a sacred institution for continuing the family lineage and pleasing the gods and ancestors. Common practices and values include:

- Marriage is a union of two families, not just individuals
- Polygamy (a man marrying multiple wives) has been traditionally accepted
- The husband/father is considered the head of the household
- Couples perform rituals and sacrifices to seek blessings and fertility from gods/ancestors
- Divorce is typically handled within the extended family or by traditional leaders

While specific customs vary between ethnic groups, these traditional values still shape many marriages in Ghana today, even in combination with Christian or Muslim practices. [IMAGE: A couple pouring libation at a traditional engagement ceremony]

| Religion | View of Marriage | Key Practices |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| Christianity | Sacred, lifelong union between man and woman | Wedding in church, monogamy, sacrificial love |  
| Islam | Completing half of faith, tranquility and mercy | Nikah contract, mahr, defined roles |
| African Traditional Religion | Union of families, continuing lineage | Polygamy, ancestral rituals, family involvement |

## Examples
Let's consider the story of Kwame and Abena, a young couple preparing to get married. Kwame was raised in a Methodist family, while Abena grew up in a Muslim home. As they start their life together, they have important decisions to make:

- What kind of wedding ceremony will honor both of their religious backgrounds? 
- How will they handle differences in beliefs and practices in their marriage?
- What shared values can they emphasize to build a loving, morally upright family?

By focusing on common principles like love, commitment, and faith in God, Kwame and Abena can find unity and build a strong marriage while still respecting each other's religious heritage. Discussing these issues openly and seeking guidance from trusted religious leaders can help them navigate this journey together.

## Summary
From this lesson, remember that:

- Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion all view marriage as a sacred institution
- Common values include love, faithfulness, defined roles, and pleasing God/gods
- There are also differences in specific beliefs and practices between religions
- Ghanaian couples often combine religious traditions in their marriages
- Focusing on shared moral principles is key to strong inter-religious marriages
- Open communication and mutual respect help navigate religious differences
- Religious leaders and communities provide important guidance and support for marriages

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Monogamy | Marriage between one man and one woman |
| Polygamy | Marriage involving multiple spouses, typically one man with multiple wives |
| Mahr | Mandatory gift or payment given by the groom to the bride in Islamic marriages |
| Nikah | The Islamic marriage contract |
| Dowry | Gifts or money transferred from the bride's family to the groom in some traditions |
| Covenant | A solemn agreement or promise, often between humans and God |
| Libation | Pouring out liquid as an offering to gods or ancestors in African traditional religions |
| Faithfulness | Being loyal and sexually exclusive to one's spouse |
| Sacrificial love | Putting the needs and well-being of one's spouse before your own |
| Mutual understanding | Making an effort to respect and appreciate each other's perspectives and beliefs |
