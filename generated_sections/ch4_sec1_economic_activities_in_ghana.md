By understanding the roles of agriculture, industry, and services, students will gain a deeper appreciation for the challenges and opportunities faced by Ghana in its quest for sustainable economic development.

## Main Content
### Agriculture: The Backbone of Ghana's Economy
Agriculture is the foundation of Ghana's economy, employing a significant portion of the workforce and contributing to the country's GDP. The main agricultural activities include:
- Crop cultivation (e.g., cocoa, maize, rice, yams)
- Livestock rearing (e.g., cattle, poultry, sheep)
- Fishing and aquaculture

### Industrial Sector: Driving Economic Transformation
The industrial sector plays a crucial role in Ghana's economic transformation, focusing on:
1. Manufacturing (e.g., textiles, food processing, cement production)
2. Mining (e.g., gold, bauxite, diamonds)
3. Construction
4. Energy production

**Key industrial hubs** in Ghana include Tema, Accra, and Kumasi.

[IMAGE: Workers at a textile factory in Tema, Ghana]

### Services Sector: The Emerging Powerhouse
The services sector has been growing rapidly in recent years, contributing significantly to Ghana's economic growth. Key service industries include:
- Financial services (e.g., banking, insurance)
- Telecommunications
- Tourism and hospitality
- Transportation and logistics

The government has been promoting the growth of the services sector through various initiatives, such as the development of technology parks and the expansion of digital infrastructure.

## Examples
1. Cocoa Production: Ghana is the second-largest cocoa producer in the world. The cocoa industry employs millions of Ghanaians and generates significant foreign exchange earnings for the country.

2. Oil and Gas: The discovery of oil and gas reserves in Ghana has boosted the country's industrial sector. The Jubilee Oil Field, located off the coast of the Western Region, has been a major contributor to Ghana's economic growth since its discovery in 2007.

## Summary
- Ghana's economy is driven by three main sectors: agriculture, industry, and services.
- Agriculture is the backbone of Ghana's economy, with cocoa being a major export crop.
- The industrial sector focuses on manufacturing, mining, construction, and energy production.
- The services sector is an emerging powerhouse, with growth in financial services, telecommunications, tourism, and transportation.
- The government plays a crucial role in promoting economic growth and development through various initiatives and policies.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term                | Definition                                                                                  |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Gross Domestic Product (GDP) | The total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific period. |
| Exports             | Goods and services produced in one country and sold to buyers in another country.           |
| Imports             | Goods and services bought from other countries.                                             |
| Manufacturing       | The production of goods from raw materials using manual labor or machines.                  |
| Service Industry    | Economic activities that provide intangible goods, such as banking, healthcare, or tourism. |
| Foreign Exchange    | The exchange of one currency for another, facilitating international trade.                 |
| Technology Park     | An area designed to promote innovation and technological development.                       |
| Digital Infrastructure | The foundational services, such as internet connectivity, that enable digital technologies. |
