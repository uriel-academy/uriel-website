## Main Content
### Components of a Job Application
A typical job application consists of several key components:
1. **Cover Letter**: A brief introduction highlighting your interest in the position and why you're a strong candidate.
2. **Resume** or **Curriculum Vitae (CV)**: A summary of your education, work experience, skills, and achievements.
3. **Application Form**: A standardized document requiring you to provide personal information, education, and work history.
4. **References**: Contact information for people who can vouch for your character, skills, and work ethic.

### Writing a Compelling Cover Letter
A cover letter is your opportunity to showcase your personality, enthusiasm, and fit for the role. Follow these tips to create a standout cover letter:
1. Research the company and tailor your letter to the specific job requirements.
2. Open with a strong hook that demonstrates your interest and qualifications.
3. Highlight your most relevant skills and experiences using specific examples.
4. Use a professional tone and proofread carefully for errors.

### Crafting a Strong Resume or CV
Your resume or CV should provide a clear and concise overview of your qualifications. Keep these points in mind:
1. Start with a summary statement that encapsulates your professional goals and strengths.
2. Organize your work experience and education in reverse-chronological order.
3. Use bullet points to highlight your achievements and responsibilities.
4. Tailor your resume to the specific job by emphasizing relevant skills and experiences.

| Resume Dos | Resume Don'ts |
|------------|---------------|
| Use a clear, professional format | Include irrelevant personal information |
| Tailor content to the job description | Use excessive jargon or abbreviations |
| Highlight accomplishments with data | Exceed two pages in length |
| Proofread for errors and consistency | Lie or exaggerate about qualifications |

### Completing the Application Form
When filling out a job application form, follow these guidelines:
1. Read instructions carefully and provide all requested information.
2. Use neat, legible handwriting or type if submitting electronically.
3. Be honest and accurate about your qualifications and experience.
4. Double-check for errors before submitting the form.

| Personal Information | Education | Work Experience | Skills & Certifications |
|----------------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------------|
| Name & Contact Info | Schools Attended | Previous Jobs & Duties | Language Proficiencies |
| Date of Birth | Dates of Attendance | Employment Dates | Technical Skills |
| Nationality | Degrees or Diplomas | Reason for Leaving | Professional Certifications |

## Practical Activity
### Activity 1: Tailoring Your Cover Letter
Materials:
- Job advertisement for a local tailor or seamstress position
- Pen and paper or computer with word processing software

Instructions:
1. Review the job advertisement and identify the key requirements and responsibilities.
2. Draft a cover letter tailored to the position, highlighting your relevant skills and experience with sewing, measuring, and working with local fabrics like kente and batik.
3. Share your cover letter with a classmate for feedback and revise as needed.

### Activity 2: Creating a Resume for a Food Service Position
Materials:
- List of your education, work experience, and skills
- Pen and paper or computer with word processing software

Instructions:
1. Imagine you are applying for a job as a chef or food vendor specializing in Ghanaian dishes like jollof rice or waakye.
2. Create a resume emphasizing your experience with food preparation, customer service, and local cuisine.
3. Organize your resume into clear sections and use bullet points to highlight your accomplishments.
4. Exchange resumes with a classmate and provide constructive feedback on format and content.
