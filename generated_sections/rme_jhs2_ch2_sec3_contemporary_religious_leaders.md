## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify at least 3 contemporary religious leaders from Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion in Ghana
2. Explain the key teachings and moral values promoted by each religious leader
3. Discuss how these religious leaders serve as role models for Ghanaian youth
4. Analyze how the examples set by these leaders can promote peace and understanding in Ghana's multi-religious society

## Introduction
Kofi and Ama, two JHS 2 students from Accra, were walking home from school one day discussing their upcoming Religious and Moral Education project. They needed to research contemporary religious leaders who serve as role models in Ghana's diverse religious landscape. As they passed by churches and mosques in their neighborhood, they realized the importance of learning from the examples set by these leaders. 

In Ghana, religious leaders play a crucial role in shaping the moral character of their followers and promoting peace in society. By studying the lives and teachings of contemporary religious leaders from Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion, students like Kofi and Ama can gain valuable insights into how to live ethically and contribute positively to their communities.

## Main Content
### Christian Leaders
One prominent Christian leader in Ghana is **Archbishop Duncan-Williams**, the founder of Action Chapel International. He is known for his teachings on faith, prayer, and moral living. Another influential leader is **Pastor Mensa Otabil**, the founder of International Central Gospel Church. He emphasizes the importance of education, hard work, and integrity.

[IMAGE: Photo of Archbishop Duncan-Williams preaching at a large gathering]

### Islamic Leaders
In the Islamic community, leaders like **Sheikh Osman Nuhu Sharubutu**, the National Chief Imam of Ghana, serve as role models. He is recognized for his efforts in promoting peace and interfaith dialogue. Another respected leader is **Alhaji Yusif Amankwah**, the Ashanti Regional Chief Imam, who advocates for education and moral uprightness among Muslim youth.

### Traditional African Religious Leaders
Traditional African Religion also has influential leaders, such as **Togbe Afede XIV**, the Agbogbomefia of the Asogli State in the Volta Region. He is known for his wisdom and guidance in preserving cultural values and promoting moral living. Another respected leader is **Nana Addo Dankwa III**, the Okuapemhene of Akuapem Traditional Area, who emphasizes the importance of honesty, responsibility, and community service.

[IMAGE: Photo of a traditional chief pouring libation at a cultural ceremony]

These religious leaders share common teachings on moral values such as:
- Integrity
- Compassion
- Respect for others
- Responsibility to the community

By following their examples, Ghanaian students can learn to:
1. Develop strong moral character
2. Contribute positively to society
3. Promote peace and understanding among people of different faiths

## Examples
1. Kwame, a student from Kumasi, was inspired by Archbishop Duncan-Williams' teachings on prayer. He started a prayer group in his school to encourage his classmates to develop their spiritual lives and support each other in living morally upright lives.

## Summary
Key takeaways from this lesson:
- Contemporary religious leaders in Ghana serve as role models for moral living and character development
- These leaders come from diverse faith traditions, including Christianity, Islam, and Traditional African Religion
- They promote values such as integrity, compassion, respect, and responsibility to the community
- By following their examples, Ghanaian students can learn to develop strong moral character and contribute positively to society
- Studying the lives and teachings of these leaders can inspire students to promote peace and understanding in Ghana's multi-religious society

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Contemporary | Belonging to or occurring in the present time |
| Role model | A person whose behavior, example, or success is emulated by others, especially younger people |
| Interfaith dialogue | Communication and positive interaction between people of different religious faiths |
| Moral uprightness | Adherence to high moral standards and principles |
| Integrity | The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles |
| Compassion | Sympathy and concern for the sufferings or misfortunes of others |
| Libation | The act of pouring a liquid offering as a religious ritual in Traditional African Religion |
| Agbogbomefia | The title given to the traditional ruler of the Asogli State in the Volta Region of Ghana |
| Okuapemhene | The title given to the traditional ruler of the Akuapem Traditional Area in the Eastern Region of Ghana |
