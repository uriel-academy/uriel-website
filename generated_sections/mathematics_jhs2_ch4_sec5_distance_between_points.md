## Main Content
### The Cartesian Coordinate System
The Cartesian coordinate system, named after the mathematician Ren√© Descartes, is a way to describe the position of points on a flat surface called a **coordinate plane**. The coordinate plane consists of two perpendicular lines called the **x-axis** (horizontal) and the **y-axis** (vertical), which intersect at a point called the **origin**. The origin has the coordinates (0, 0).

[IMAGE: A coordinate plane with the x-axis, y-axis, origin, and a point P(x, y) labeled]

### Coordinates of Points
Any point on the coordinate plane can be described by an ordered pair of numbers **(x, y)**, where **x** represents the distance from the origin along the x-axis, and **y** represents the distance from the origin along the y-axis. The x-value is called the **abscissa**, and the y-value is called the **ordinate**.

To find the coordinates of a point:
1. Start at the origin (0, 0)
2. Move horizontally along the x-axis (right for positive values, left for negative values)
3. Move vertically along the y-axis (up for positive values, down for negative values)

### The Distance Formula
The distance between two points on a coordinate plane can be calculated using the **distance formula**. For two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, the distance $d$ between them is given by:

$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

This formula is derived from the Pythagorean theorem, which states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the longest side) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

[IMAGE: A right-angled triangle on a coordinate plane with the horizontal and vertical sides labeled as $(x_2 - x_1)$ and $(y_2 - y_1)$, and the hypotenuse labeled as $d$]

### Properties of the Distance Formula
The distance formula has the following properties:
1. Distance is always positive or zero
2. Distance is symmetric, meaning the distance from point A to point B is the same as the distance from point B to point A
3. The distance between a point and itself is zero

| Property | Mathematical Expression |
|----------|-------------------------|
| Non-negativity | $d \geq 0$ |
| Symmetry | $d(A, B) = d(B, A)$ |
| Zero distance | $d(A, A) = 0$ |

## Worked Examples
### Example 1
Find the distance between the points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7).

Solution:
Let's use the distance formula with $(x_1, y_1) = (2, 3)$ and $(x_2, y_2) = (5, 7)$.

$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$
$d = \sqrt{(5 - 2)^2 + (7 - 3)^2}$
$d = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2}$
$d = \sqrt{9 + 16}$
$d = \sqrt{25}$
$d = 5$

Therefore, the distance between points A and B is 5 units.

### Example 2
Kofi wants to calculate the distance between his house at (3, 4) and his friend Ama's house at (-2, 1) on a coordinate plane where each unit represents 100 meters. How far apart are their houses?

Solution:
Let's use the distance formula with $(x_1, y_1) = (3, 4)$ and $(x_2, y_2) = (-2, 1)$.

$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$
$d = \sqrt{(-2 - 3)^2 + (1 - 4)^2}$
$d = \sqrt{(-5)^2 + (-3)^2}$
$d = \sqrt{25 + 9}$
$d = \sqrt{34}$
$d \approx 5.83$

Since each unit represents 100 meters, the actual distance is:
$5.83 \times 100 = 583$ meters

Therefore, Kofi's house and Ama's house are approximately 583 meters apart.

### Example 3
A farmer in Kumasi has a rectangular plot of land with corners at (0, 0), (0, 6), (8, 6), and (8, 0). Find the length and width of the plot.

[IMAGE: A rectangular plot on a coordinate plane with the corners labeled]

Solution:
To find the length and width, we need to calculate the distances between the appropriate pairs of corners.

Length (distance between (0, 0) and (8, 0)):
$d = \sqrt{(8 - 0)^2 + (0 - 0)^2} = \sqrt{64 + 0} = \sqrt{64} = 8$

Width (distance between (0, 0) and (0, 6)):
$d = \sqrt{(0 - 0)^2 + (6 - 0)^2} = \sqrt{0 + 36} = \sqrt{36} = 6$

Therefore, the length of the plot is 8 units, and the width is 6 units.

## Summary
- The Cartesian coordinate system consists of a coordinate plane with an x-axis, y-axis, and origin.
- Points on the coordinate plane are described by ordered pairs (x, y) called coordinates.
- The distance between two points can be calculated using the distance formula: $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$.
- The distance formula is derived from the Pythagorean theorem.
- Distance is always non-negative, symmetric, and zero between a point and itself.
- The distance formula can be used to solve real-world problems involving distance, such as in farming, market calculations, and transportation.
