## Main Content
### Organs of the Digestive System
The digestive system consists of several organs that work together to process food:
1. **Mouth**: The mouth is where digestion begins. Teeth mechanically break down food, while saliva contains enzymes that start to chemically digest carbohydrates.
2. **Esophagus**: The esophagus is a muscular tube that transports food from the mouth to the stomach.
3. **Stomach**: The stomach is a muscular sac that churns food and secretes acid and enzymes to further break down food.
4. **Small intestine**: The small intestine is where most digestion and absorption of nutrients occurs. It is divided into three parts: duodenum, jejunum, and ileum.
5. **Large intestine**: The large intestine, or colon, absorbs water and forms feces from undigested food.
6. **Rectum and anus**: The rectum stores feces before elimination through the anus.

### Processes of Digestion
Digestion involves four main processes:
1. **Ingestion**: Taking food into the mouth
2. **Digestion**: Breaking down food into smaller molecules through mechanical and chemical means
3. **Absorption**: Transporting nutrients from the digestive tract into the bloodstream
4. **Egestion**: Eliminating undigested food as feces

### Role of Enzymes
Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in the body. In digestion, enzymes break down large food molecules into smaller ones that can be absorbed. The main digestive enzymes are:
- **Amylase**: Breaks down carbohydrates (found in saliva and pancreatic juice)
- **Pepsin**: Breaks down proteins (found in stomach)
- **Lipase**: Breaks down fats (found in pancreatic juice)
- **Trypsin**: Breaks down proteins (found in pancreatic juice)

### Practical Experiment: Enzyme Action on Starch
Materials:
- Cooked cassava or yam
- Iodine solution
- Saliva
- Test tubes or clear containers

Procedure:
1. Mash a small piece of cooked cassava or yam in a test tube.
2. Add a few drops of iodine solution to the mashed food. The presence of starch will turn the iodine blue-black.
3. In another test tube, collect some saliva (make sure not to contaminate it with food particles).
4. Add the saliva to the mashed food and mix well.
5. After a few minutes, add a few drops of iodine solution to the mixture.
6. Observe the color change. If the iodine remains brown, it indicates that the amylase in saliva has broken down the starch.

This experiment demonstrates the action of the enzyme amylase in saliva on the starch present in cassava or yam.

## Summary
- The main organs of the digestive system are the mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum, and anus.
- Digestion involves ingestion, digestion, absorption, and egestion.
- Enzymes are biological catalysts that break down food molecules during digestion.
- The main digestive enzymes are amylase, pepsin, lipase, and trypsin.
- A balanced diet is essential for maintaining a healthy digestive system.
- Practical experiments can demonstrate the action of digestive enzymes.

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Ingestion | The process of taking food into the mouth |
| Digestion | The process of breaking down food into smaller molecules |
| Absorption | The process of transporting nutrients from the digestive tract into the bloodstream |
| Egestion | The process of eliminating undigested food as feces |
| Enzyme | A biological catalyst that speeds up chemical reactions in the body |
| Amylase | An enzyme that breaks down carbohydrates |
| Pepsin | An enzyme that breaks down proteins in the stomach |
| Lipase | An enzyme that breaks down fats |
| Trypsin | An enzyme that breaks down proteins in the small intestine |
| Peristalsis | Rhythmic muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract |
| Villi | Finger-like projections in the small intestine that increase surface area for absorption |
| Rectum | The last part of the large intestine where feces are stored before elimination |
| Anus | The opening at the end of the digestive tract through which feces are eliminated |
| Balanced diet | A diet that includes a variety of foods in the right proportions to maintain health |
| Calorie | A unit of energy in food (1 calorie = 4.184 joules) |
