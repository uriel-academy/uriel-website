## Main Content
### Common Food Safety Hazards
Food safety hazards are factors that can cause harm to consumers if not properly controlled. These hazards can be classified into three main categories:
1. **Biological hazards**: Bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungi that can cause foodborne illnesses
2. **Chemical hazards**: Pesticides, cleaning agents, and other harmful substances that can contaminate food
3. **Physical hazards**: Foreign objects such as glass, metal, or plastic that can accidentally enter food during preparation

To prevent these hazards, it is essential to maintain a clean and sanitary environment, handle food properly, and cook ingredients to safe temperatures.

### Personal Hygiene and Protective Equipment
Maintaining personal hygiene is crucial for food handlers to prevent the spread of harmful bacteria and viruses. Key practices include:
- Proper handwashing technique: Wash hands with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds before handling food, after using the restroom, and after touching raw ingredients
- Use of protective equipment: Wear clean aprons, hairnets, and gloves when preparing food to minimize the risk of contamination

### Safety Precautions
When working with kitchen equipment and hot surfaces, it is crucial to take safety precautions to avoid accidents and injuries. Always follow these safety rules:
- Handle knives and sharp objects with care, keeping fingers away from blades
- Use oven mitts or potholders when handling hot pots, pans, and baking sheets
- Turn pot and pan handles inward to prevent accidental knocking
- Keep flammable items, such as towels and curtains, away from stovetops and open flames
- Have a fire extinguisher readily available and know how to use it properly

## Practical Activity
### Preparing a Safe and Hygienic Fruit Salad
Materials needed:
- 2-3 locally available fruits (e.g., mango, pineapple, papaya)
- Cutting board
- Sharp knife
- Bowl for mixing
- Apron and hairnet

Instructions:
1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds.
2. Put on a clean apron and hairnet.
3. Wash the fruits under running water, scrubbing gently to remove any dirt or debris.
4. Place the washed fruits on a clean cutting board.
5. Using a clean, sharp knife, carefully cut the fruits into bite-sized pieces, discarding any seeds or inedible parts.
6. Place the cut fruit pieces into a clean mixing bowl.
7. Gently toss the fruit pieces to combine.
8. Serve the fruit salad immediately, or cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Expected outcomes:
- A delicious and visually appealing fruit salad
- Demonstrated understanding of proper handwashing, protective equipment, and safe food preparation techniques

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Food safety | The practice of handling, preparing, and storing food in a way that prevents foodborne illnesses |
| Hygiene | Conditions and practices that promote health and prevent the spread of diseases |
| Biological hazard | Living organisms, such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungi, that can cause foodborne illnesses |
| Chemical hazard | Harmful substances, such as pesticides and cleaning agents, that can contaminate food |
| Physical hazard | Foreign objects, such as glass or metal, that can accidentally enter food during preparation |
| Cross-contamination | The transfer of harmful bacteria from one food or surface to another |
| Perishable | Food items that spoil quickly and require refrigeration to maintain freshness and safety |
| Foodborne illness | Sickness caused by consuming contaminated food or beverages |
| Sanitize | To clean surfaces or equipment in a way that reduces harmful bacteria to safe levels |
| Contamination | The presence of harmful substances or organisms in food that can cause illness or injury |
