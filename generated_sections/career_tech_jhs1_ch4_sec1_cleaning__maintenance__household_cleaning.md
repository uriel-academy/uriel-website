### Sweeping Techniques
Sweeping is a basic but essential cleaning skill. Follow these steps for effective sweeping:
1. Start from the farthest corner of the room and work your way towards the door.
2. Use short, overlapping strokes to gather dirt and debris into a pile.
3. Pay extra attention to corners and edges where dust tends to accumulate.
4. Collect the pile of dirt using a dustpan and dispose of it properly.

[IMAGE: A person demonstrating proper sweeping technique]

### Mopping and Floor Cleaning
After sweeping, mopping helps remove stubborn dirt and sanitizes your floors. Here's how to mop effectively:
1. Fill your bucket with warm water and add a cleaning solution (see homemade recipe below).
2. Dip your mop into the bucket and wring out excess water.
3. Start mopping from the farthest corner of the room, working your way towards the door.
4. Use a figure-eight motion to cover the entire floor surface.
5. Rinse your mop frequently and change the water when it becomes dirty.

### Homemade Cleaning Solutions
You don't always need expensive store-bought cleaners to keep your home clean. Try this simple homemade cleaning solution using locally available ingredients:

| Ingredient   | Amount   |
|--------------|----------|
| Warm water   | 4 cups   |
| White vinegar| 1/2 cup  |
| Lemon juice  | 1/4 cup  |
| Baking soda  | 2 tbsp   |

Mix all ingredients together in a spray bottle and use it to clean various surfaces like countertops, sinks, and tiles.

## Practical Activity
Now that you've learned the basics of household cleaning, let's put your skills to the test!

Activity 1: Classroom Cleanup
- Materials: Brooms, dustpans, mops, buckets, cleaning cloths, homemade cleaning solution
- Instructions:
  1. Divide the class into teams and assign each team a section of the classroom.
  2. Have each team sweep their assigned area, collecting dirt and debris in a dustpan.
  3. Prepare the homemade cleaning solution and fill buckets with water.
  4. Each team should mop their section of the classroom using the techniques learned.
  5. Use cleaning cloths to dust surfaces like desks, shelves, and window sills.
- Expected outcome: A clean and tidy classroom that everyone can take pride in.

Activity 2: Develop a Weekly Cleaning Schedule
- Materials: Paper, pens, or pencils
- Instructions:
  1. Have students create a table with days of the week as rows and cleaning tasks as columns.
  2. Fill in the table, assigning specific cleaning tasks to each day (e.g., Monday: sweep and mop, Tuesday: dust furniture, etc.).
  3. Encourage students to follow their cleaning schedule at home and share their experiences with the class.
- Expected outcome: Students develop a habit of regular cleaning and maintain a tidy home environment.
