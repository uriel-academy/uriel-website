# Chapter 2: Sewing & Clothing
## Section: Garment Construction - Complex Patterns

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Take accurate body measurements for complex garments
2. Draft and cut patterns for complex designs like kaba and slit, agbada, and kente designs
3. Sew darts, pleats, and gathers to create shape and fullness
4. Attach facings, linings, and interfacing neatly
5. Construct and attach collars, sleeves, and cuffs
6. Finish garments with hems, closures, and embellishments

## Introduction
Ghanaian fashion is known worldwide for its vibrant colors, bold patterns, and unique designs. From the elegant kaba and slit worn by women to the stately agbada donned by men, our traditional garments showcase the skill and creativity of local tailors and seamstresses. Constructing these complex garments requires advanced sewing techniques and an eye for detail. Whether you aspire to start your own tailoring business like Naadu's Fashion in Accra, work as a seamstress in the bustling Kumasi Central Market, or design stylish outfits for special occasions, mastering the art of complex garment construction will open doors to exciting career opportunities in Ghana's thriving fashion industry.

## Main Content

### Taking Accurate Measurements
Before drafting patterns for complex garments, it's crucial to take precise body measurements. Use a flexible tape measure and record measurements in centimeters. For kaba and slit, measure bust, waist, hips, back length, and skirt length. For agbada, measure chest, shoulder width, sleeve length, and garment length. Remember to add ease for comfort and movement.

### Drafting and Cutting Complex Patterns
Using the measurements, draft patterns on brown paper or pattern paper. **Add seam allowances** and mark **notches** and **grain lines**. Carefully pin patterns to fabric, ensuring the grain line is straight. Cut fabric pieces accurately, using sharp scissors and following the pattern outline. Transfer markings like darts and pleats.

[IMAGE: Drafting kaba and slit pattern pieces with key markings]

### Shaping Garments with Darts, Pleats, and Gathers
Create shape and fullness in garments using darts, pleats, and gathers. Sew **darts** by folding fabric right sides together, stitching from wide end to point, and tying off. Form **pleats** by folding fabric and securing with pins before stitching. For **gathers**, sew two parallel lines of basting stitches, then pull bobbin threads to gather fabric evenly.

[IMAGE: Demonstrating dart, pleat, and gather techniques on sample fabric pieces]

### Attaching Facings, Linings, and Interfacing
Finish garment edges and add structure with facings, linings, and interfacing. Cut facings and linings from the same fabric as the garment, and interfacing from a suitable weight. Attach facings to necklines and armholes, ensuring smooth curves. Sew linings to facings and hem separately. Fuse or sew interfacing to garment pieces for stability.

### Constructing Collars, Sleeves, and Cuffs
Elevate garments with well-constructed collars, sleeves, and cuffs. Sew collar pieces right sides together, turn right side out, and press. Attach collar to neckline, matching notches and easing curved edges. Set in sleeves, gathering sleeve caps if needed. Attach cuffs to sleeve hems, using interfacing for crisp edges.

| Garment Part | Construction Steps |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Collars      | 1. Sew collar pieces right sides together<br>2. Turn right side out and press<br>3. Attach to neckline, matching notches |
| Sleeves      | 1. Gather sleeve cap if needed<br>2. Set in sleeves, easing sleeve cap<br>3. Sew sleeve seam and hem |
| Cuffs        | 1. Interface cuff pieces<br>2. Sew cuff pieces right sides together<br>3. Attach to sleeve hem |

### Finishing Touches: Hems, Closures, and Embellishments
Complete garments with neat hems, secure closures, and decorative embellishments. Hem skirts and sleeves by turning up the fabric twice and stitching. Attach zippers, buttons, or hooks and eyes for closures. Add embellishments like embroidery, appliqué, or beading to showcase your creativity and style.

| Finishing Step   | Procedure |
|------------------|-----------|
| Hemming          | 1. Turn up fabric edge twice<br>2. Pin and press<br>3. Stitch hem in place |
| Attaching Zippers| 1. Pin zipper to seam allowance<br>2. Sew zipper using zipper foot<br>3. Secure ends and test zipper |
| Adding Embellishments | 1. Mark placement of embellishments<br>2. Sew or attach securely<br>3. Ensure neat and even distribution |

## Practical Activity
1. Draft a pattern for a simple kaba top with darts and a faced neckline
   - Materials: Brown paper, pencil, ruler, tape measure, fabric, sewing notions
   - Instructions:
     1. Take bust, waist, and back length measurements
     2. Draft front and back bodice pieces on brown paper, adding darts and seam allowances
     3. Cut out pattern pieces and pin to fabric
     4. Cut fabric pieces and transfer markings
     5. Sew darts, shoulder seams, and side seams
     6. Attach facing to neckline and finish raw edges
   - Expected outcome: A well-fitted kaba top with a faced neckline and darts for shaping

2. Construct a gathered sleeve and attach it to a bodice
   - Materials: Fabric, sewing notions, drafted sleeve pattern
   - Instructions:
     1. Cut out sleeve pieces from fabric
     2. Sew two rows of basting stitches along sleeve cap
     3. Pull bobbin threads to gather sleeve cap evenly
     4. Pin sleeve to armhole, matching notches and easing gathers
     5. Sew sleeve to armhole and finish raw edges
     6. Sew sleeve seam and hem
   - Expected outcome: A gathered sleeve attached neatly to a bodice

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Seam allowance | Extra fabric added to pattern pieces for joining |
| Notches | Small cuts on pattern edges for matching pieces |
| Grain line | Arrow indicating the direction of fabric grain |
| Dart | Stitched fold for shaping garments |
| Pleat | Folded and stitched fabric for decoration or fullness |
| Gather | Evenly distributed fabric fullness |
| Facing | Piece of fabric used to finish raw edges |
| Lining | Fabric sewn inside a garment for comfort and finishing |
| Interfacing | Material attached to fabric for stability and structure |
| Appliqué | Decorative technique of sewing fabric shapes onto garments |
| Basting | Temporary long stitches for holding fabric in place |
| Grading | Trimming seam allowances to reduce bulk |
