## Main Content
### Common Special Dietary Needs
Some common special dietary needs include:
- Diabetic diets: Low in sugar and carbohydrates to manage blood glucose levels
- Lactose-intolerant diets: Avoid dairy products due to difficulty digesting lactose
- Vegetarian diets: Exclude meat and fish, but may include eggs and dairy
- Vegan diets: Exclude all animal products, including meat, fish, eggs, and dairy

### Planning Meals for Special Diets
When planning meals for people with special dietary needs, consider the following:
1. Nutrient balance: Ensure meals include a variety of nutrients from different food groups
2. Portion control: Adjust portion sizes based on individual needs and dietary restrictions
3. Ingredient substitutions: Replace restricted ingredients with suitable alternatives
4. Cooking methods: Choose healthy cooking methods like grilling, steaming, or baking

### Modifying Recipes for Special Diets
To modify recipes for special diets:
1. Identify ingredients that need to be replaced or omitted
2. Research suitable substitutions for restricted ingredients
3. Adjust quantities of ingredients to maintain taste and texture
4. Test the modified recipe and make further adjustments if needed

### Preparing Meals for Diabetic and Lactose-Intolerant Diets
When preparing meals for diabetic and lactose-intolerant diets:
- Choose low glycemic index (GI) foods for diabetic diets
- Use lactose-free milk or dairy alternatives for lactose-intolerant diets
- Include plenty of vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats
- Limit processed foods, added sugars, and saturated fats

Safety Precautions:
- Always check food labels for allergens and restricted ingredients
- Use separate utensils and cooking surfaces to avoid cross-contamination
- Ensure proper food storage and reheating to prevent foodborne illness

## Practical Activity
1. Plan a balanced meal for a diabetic family member using local Ghanaian ingredients
   - Materials: Pen, paper, local grocery items
   - Instructions:
     1. Research low GI Ghanaian foods
     2. Create a menu including a lean protein, non-starchy vegetables, and a whole grain
     3. Write out the recipe, including cooking methods and portion sizes
   - Expected outcome: A well-balanced, diabetic-friendly meal plan using Ghanaian ingredients
2. Prepare a lactose-free version of a traditional Ghanaian dish, such as groundnut soup
   - Materials: Lactose-free milk or coconut milk, groundnuts, tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, salt, pepper
   - Instructions:
     1. Soak and blend groundnuts to make a paste
     2. Saut√© onions, garlic, and ginger in a pot
     3. Add tomatoes and groundnut paste, stirring well
     4. Pour in lactose-free milk or coconut milk and simmer until thickened
     5. Season with salt and pepper to taste
   - Expected outcome: A delicious, lactose-free version of groundnut soup
