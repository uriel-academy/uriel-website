## Main Content

### What are Stealing and Cheating? 
**Stealing** is taking something that rightfully belongs to someone else without their permission. This could be taking an object, money, or even someone's intellectual property, like copying their work. **Cheating** is acting dishonestly to gain an unfair advantage, often by breaking rules. Examples include cheating on tests, in games or sports, or in a relationship by being unfaithful.

### Religious Teachings Against Stealing and Cheating
All major religions in Ghana condemn stealing and cheating as immoral acts:

- **Christianity**: The Bible includes "Thou shalt not steal" as one of the Ten Commandments (Exodus 20:15). Proverbs 12:22 says "Lying lips are abomination to the Lord: but they that deal truly are his delight." Jesus taught his followers to love others and treat them with honesty.

- **Islam**: The Quran instructs "And do not consume one another's wealth unjustly or send it [in bribery] to the rulers in order that [they might aid] you [to] consume a portion of the wealth of the people in sin, while you know [it is unlawful]." (2:188) Islam emphasizes truthfulness and integrity.

- **Traditional African Religion**: Many traditional Ghanaian values and folktales warn against stealing and cheating. Honesty is seen as essential for social harmony. Stealing and cheating are thought to bring curses and misfortune on the offender and community.

### Consequences of Stealing and Cheating
Stealing and cheating have negative impacts on individuals and society:

- Loss of trust and damaged relationships
- Unfair treatment of victims
- Punishment like fines or imprisonment  
- Guilt and loss of self-respect
- Bad role model for others
- Breakdown of social order if widespread

### Resisting Temptation and Living with Integrity
To avoid stealing or cheating:

1. Remember your religious and moral values. Ask yourself if God would approve.
2. Think about people who could be harmed, including yourself. 
3. Find honest ways to get what you need - ask for help if in difficult circumstances.
4. Surround yourself with positive influences and good role models.
5. Have courage to do the right thing, even if others are doing wrong.

## Examples
1. Abena noticed the shopkeeper gave her too much change by mistake. She was tempted to keep the extra money, but remembered Islam teaches honesty. She returned the extra change to the grateful shopkeeper. 

2. During his BECE exams, Kwame saw some classmates trying to share answers. Though nervous about his scores, he chose not to participate in the cheating. He prayed for success and focused on doing his own best.

3. Kofi's elderly neighbor had a beautiful mango tree. Though no one would see him take the fruit, Kofi resisted the temptation to steal. He knew traditional wisdom warned theft brings misfortune. He felt good about his integrity.

## Summary
Key takeaways about stealing and cheating:

- Stealing is taking something belonging to another without permission; cheating is acting dishonestly for unfair advantage
- Christianity, Islam and Traditional African Religion all condemn stealing and cheating as sins
- Stealing and cheating bring negative consequences like damaged relationships, punishment, guilt, and social harms
- We can resist temptations by remembering religious teachings, considering harms, finding honest alternatives, choosing good influences, and having moral courage
- Living with honesty and integrity brings self-respect, trust and blessings
