### Fixing Leaky Taps and Pipes
Leaky taps and pipes are common plumbing issues that can waste water and damage your property. To fix a leaky tap:

1. Turn off the water supply to the tap
2. Remove the tap handle and unscrew the tap bonnet
3. Replace the worn washer or O-ring
4. Reassemble the tap and turn the water supply back on
5. Test the tap to ensure the leak is fixed

[IMAGE: Step-by-step demonstration of replacing a tap washer]

For leaky pipes, locate the source of the leak and tighten any loose fittings using an adjustable wrench. If the leak persists, you may need to replace the damaged section of pipe.

### Clearing Clogged Drains
Clogged drains can cause water to drain slowly or not at all. To clear a clogged drain:

1. Remove any visible debris from the drain opening
2. Pour a mixture of boiling water and baking soda down the drain
3. If the clog persists, use a plunger or drain snake to dislodge the blockage
4. Avoid using harsh chemicals, as they can damage your pipes

[IMAGE: Demonstration of using a plunger to clear a clogged drain]

### Importance of Regular Maintenance
Regular plumbing maintenance can prevent major issues and extend the life of your plumbing system. Some key maintenance tasks include:

- Checking for leaks and repairing them promptly
- Cleaning drain openings and removing debris
- Insulating pipes to prevent freezing and bursting
- Replacing worn parts before they fail

By performing these tasks regularly, you can save money on costly repairs and ensure your plumbing system functions efficiently.

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Plumbing | The system of pipes, drains, and fixtures that distribute water and remove waste in a building |
| Leak | An unintended escape of water from pipes or fixtures |
| Washer | A flat, circular piece of rubber or other material used to seal connections and prevent leaks |
| O-ring | A circular seal made of rubber or other flexible material, used to prevent leaks in cylindrical connections |
| Tap bonnet | The part of a tap that covers the internal mechanism and is often used to access the washer |
| Pipe fitting | A connector used to join pipes or change their direction |
| Plunger | A tool with a rubber suction cup used to clear clogs in drains and toilets |
| Drain snake | A flexible, coiled wire used to remove tough clogs from pipes |
| Insulation | Material used to wrap pipes and prevent heat loss or freezing |
| Maintenance | Regular upkeep and repairs to keep a system functioning properly |
