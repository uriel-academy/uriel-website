## Introduction
Have you ever noticed the beautiful embroidery on a friend's dress or the intricate stitching on the kente cloth stalls at Makola Market? Those eye-catching designs are created using decorative stitches! Decorative stitches allow you to add unique flair and style to clothing, accessories, and home decor items. These stitches are used by fashion designers, tailors, and textile artisans across Ghana to elevate their creations. From outlining adinkra symbols on batik cloth to enhancing the borders of kente strips, decorative stitches play an important role in our vibrant textile traditions. Learning decorative stitches empowers you to customize your own projects, start a small sewing business, or pursue a career in fashion design, tailoring, or textile arts.

## Main Content

### Types of Decorative Stitches
There are many decorative stitches, each creating a distinct look. Some common ones include:

1. **Running Stitch**: A simple stitch of evenly spaced stitches in a straight line. Can be a single row or multiple rows.

2. **Back Stitch**: A strong stitch that doubles back on itself, ideal for outlining designs or repairing seams. 

3. **Cross Stitch**: X-shaped stitches used to form designs across a grid. Commonly used for embellishing household linens.

4. **Blanket Stitch**: A stitch that loops around the edge of fabric, often used to finish blanket edges or appliqué shapes to garments.

### How to Sew Decorative Stitches
Follow these general steps to sew decorative stitches:

1. Trace or draw your design onto the fabric using a template and fabric pencil. 
2. Place fabric in embroidery hoop, making sure it is taut and secure.
3. Thread your needle with embroidery floss and tie a knot at the end.
4. Begin stitching your design, following the stitch pattern instructions, keeping stitches even.
5. Tie off thread securely when design is complete. 
6. Remove fabric from hoop and press design on wrong side.

Safety Precautions:
- Always point needle away from your body while stitching.
- Keep scissors closed and store them safely when not in use.
- Avoid leaving needles or pins loose to prevent accidents.

### Incorporating Ghanaian Adinkra and Kente Motifs
Elevate your decorative stitches by incorporating traditional Ghanaian adinkra symbols and kente patterns:

- Choose adinkra symbols that resonate with you, like Nyame Dua (tree of God) for faith or Sesa Wo Suban (change your character) for transformation. Outline them in back stitch or cross stitch.
- Create borders inspired by kente cloth patterns, alternating bands of running stitch in different colors. The Babadua pattern, for example, features arrow-like shapes.

## Practical Activity
Let's put your new decorative stitch skills to use! 

### Adinkra Symbol Embroidery Sampler
Materials:
- 30cm square of cotton fabric 
- Embroidery hoop
- Embroidery floss in 3 colors
- Adinkra symbol template
- Embroidery needle
- Fabric pencil

Instructions:
1. Choose 3 adinkra symbols and lay out design on paper.
2. Trace adinkra symbols onto fabric using template and fabric pencil. 
3. Place fabric in embroidery hoop.
4. Stitch each symbol in a different color and stitch type:
   - Stitaafi (learning and wisdom) in green cross stitch 
   - Akoma (the heart) in red back stitch
   - Dwennimmen (ram's horns) in yellow running stitch
5. Remove fabric from hoop, tie off all threads.
6. Press sampler and display your work!
