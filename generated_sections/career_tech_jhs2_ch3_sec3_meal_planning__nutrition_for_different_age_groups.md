## Main Content
### Nutritional Needs of Different Age Groups
#### Infants (0-12 months)
- Infants require breast milk or formula as their primary source of nutrition
- From 6 months, introduce complementary foods such as pureed fruits, vegetables, and fortified porridge
- Iron-rich foods like mashed beans and green leafy vegetables are important for preventing anemia

#### Children (1-12 years)
- Children need a balanced diet with a variety of foods from all food groups
- Include plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats
- Limit intake of sugary drinks and snacks

#### Adolescents (13-18 years)
- Adolescents require increased energy and nutrients to support rapid growth and development
- Emphasize calcium-rich foods like milk, yogurt, and leafy greens for strong bones
- Provide iron-rich foods such as meat, fish, and legumes to prevent anemia

#### Adults (19-59 years)
- Adults need a balanced diet to maintain health and prevent chronic diseases
- Include a variety of nutrient-dense foods while limiting processed and high-fat foods
- Engage in regular physical activity to maintain a healthy weight

#### Elderly (60+ years)
- The elderly may require fewer calories but more nutrient-dense foods
- Focus on foods rich in calcium, vitamin D, and fiber to support bone health and digestion
- Ensure adequate protein intake to maintain muscle mass

### Planning Balanced Meals
To plan balanced meals for each age group, follow these steps:
1. Include foods from all the major food groups in the appropriate proportions
2. Choose nutrient-dense foods over empty calories
3. Consider the specific nutritional needs of each age group
4. Use locally available, affordable, and culturally appropriate ingredients

### Food Safety and Hygiene
When preparing meals, it is crucial to follow proper food safety and hygiene practices to prevent foodborne illnesses:

| Safety Rule | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Wash hands  | Wash hands with soap and water before handling food |
| Clean surfaces | Keep kitchen surfaces and utensils clean and sanitized |
| Separate raw and cooked foods | Avoid cross-contamination by keeping raw and cooked foods separate |
| Cook thoroughly | Cook foods to safe internal temperatures to kill harmful bacteria |
| Store properly | Store foods at the appropriate temperature and use within safe time limits |

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Nutrition | The process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth |
| Balanced diet | A diet that includes a variety of foods in the right proportions to meet nutritional needs |
| Food groups | Categories of foods with similar nutritional properties (e.g., fruits, vegetables, grains, proteins) |
| Nutrient-dense | Foods that provide a high amount of nutrients relative to their calorie content |
| Complementary foods | Foods introduced to infants alongside breast milk or formula, typically from 6 months of age |
| Anemia | A condition characterized by a lack of healthy red blood cells, often due to iron deficiency |
| Chronic diseases | Long-lasting health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity |
| Calories | Units of energy provided by food |
| Food safety | Practices to ensure that food is safe to eat and free from contamination |
| Foodborne illness | Sickness caused by consuming contaminated food or drink |
| Cross-contamination | The transfer of harmful bacteria from one food or surface to another |
| Sanitize | To clean and disinfect surfaces or utensils to prevent the growth of harmful bacteria |
