## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify common materials used in craft production in Ghana
2. Safely use basic tools for measuring, cutting, and shaping materials
3. Create a simple fabric item using Ghanaian fabrics like kente or batik
4. Prepare a traditional Ghanaian dish like waakye or jollof rice
5. Construct a small wooden item using carpentry techniques

## Introduction
Craft production is an important part of Ghanaian culture and economy. From the vibrant colors of kente cloth to the delicious flavors of waakye, the skills of creating useful items are all around us. These practical abilities are valuable for entrepreneurs starting their own businesses, like tailors, food sellers, and carpenters. They also help us in our daily lives, whether we're cooking a nutritious meal for our family or repairing a broken chair. In this lesson, we'll explore some of the key materials and techniques used in craft production in Ghana, and develop hands-on skills that can lead to exciting career opportunities.

### Tools and Techniques
To shape these raw materials into useful items, artisans rely on a variety of tools and techniques. Some common tools include:

- **Measuring tools**: Tape measures, rulers, calipers
- **Cutting tools**: Saws, chisels, knives, scissors
- **Shaping tools**: Hammers, planers, files, sanders
- **Joining tools**: Nails, screws, glue, thread and needles

[IMAGE: Labeled photos of key tools]

It's important to use these tools safely and properly. Always wear protective gear like gloves and goggles when necessary. Keep tools sharp and clean, and store them securely when not in use.

### Woodworking
Woodworking is another important craft in Ghana, used to make everything from furniture to cooking utensils. Some basic woodworking techniques include:

1. **Measuring and marking**: Use a ruler or tape measure and pencil to mark cut lines on the wood.

2. **Cutting**: Use a saw to carefully cut along the marked lines. Keep fingers away from the blade.

3. **Shaping**: Use a chisel, planer, or sandpaper to shape and smooth the wood pieces.

4. **Assembly**: Use nails, screws, or glue to join the pieces together securely.

## Practical Activity
Now it's your turn to put these craft production skills into practice! Choose one of the following activities:

### Fabric Placemat
Materials needed:
- Two pieces of fabric (kente, batik, or printed cotton), each 35cm x 45cm
- Scissors
- Pins
- Needle and thread

Instructions:
1. Cut the fabric pieces to the correct size (35cm x 45cm) using scissors.
2. Place the two fabric pieces together with the right sides facing each other. Pin around the edges.
3. Sew around the edges, leaving a small opening on one side.
4. Turn the placemat right side out through the opening. Press flat with your hands.
5. Sew the opening closed.

[IMAGE: Finished placemat]

### Wooden Cooking Spoon
Materials needed:
- Piece of soft wood (like cedar or mahogany) about 20cm long and 5cm wide
- Pencil
- Saw
- Chisel or knife
- Sandpaper

Instructions:
1. Draw the outline of a spoon on the wood with a pencil.
2. Carefully cut out the spoon shape with a saw.
3. Use a chisel or knife to carve out the bowl of the spoon.
4. Smooth the spoon with sandpaper until the surface is even.
5. Rub the spoon with a food-safe oil like coconut oil to protect the wood.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Kente | A colorful, woven fabric native to Ghana |
| Batik | A method of dyeing fabric using wax resist |
| Chisel | A metal tool with a sharp edge for carving wood or stone |
| Planer | A tool for smoothing and shaping wood |
| Saw | A tool with a toothed blade for cutting wood |
| Measuring tape | A flexible ruler used for measuring size or distance |
| Needle | A thin metal tool with a sharp point, used for sewing |
| Thread | A thin strand of cotton, nylon, or other material used for sewing |
| Sandpaper | Paper with abrasive material glued to its surface, used for smoothing wood |
| Food-safe oil | An edible oil like coconut or olive oil used to protect wood |
