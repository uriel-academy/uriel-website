## Learning Objectives
After completing this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Plan balanced and appropriate menus for different types of events
2. Calculate quantities of ingredients needed based on the number of people
3. Prepare a variety of Ghanaian dishes suitable for catering events
4. Set up and serve food in an attractive and hygienic manner
5. Work collaboratively in a team to cater an event

## Introduction
Catering for events is an important skill in Ghana, where celebrations like weddings, birthdays, and naming ceremonies often involve serving food to many guests. Caterers play a vital role in making these events successful and memorable. Learning how to plan menus, prepare dishes in large quantities, and serve food attractively can open up career opportunities in the hospitality industry or allow you to start your own catering business, like many successful Ghanaian entrepreneurs. Even if you don't pursue catering professionally, being able to cook for large groups is a valuable life skill that enables you to host events for family and friends.

## Main Content

### Planning Menus for Events
- Consider the type of event, number of guests, budget, and any dietary restrictions
- Plan a balanced menu with a variety of dishes, including starters, main courses, sides, and desserts
- Choose dishes that can be prepared in advance and easily served to a large group
- Example menu for a Ghanaian wedding:
  - Starter: Kelewele (spiced fried plantains)
  - Main course: Jollof rice, grilled chicken, beef stew, steamed vegetables
  - Dessert: Fresh fruit platter

### Calculating Quantities
- Determine the number of guests and estimate how much each person is likely to eat
- Use a **quantity guide** to calculate the amount of ingredients needed:

| Food Item   | Quantity per Person |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Rice        | 100-150g            |
| Stew        | 200-250ml           |
| Chicken     | 200-250g            |
| Vegetables  | 100-150g            |

- Add an extra 10-15% to account for unexpected guests or second helpings
- Make a shopping list based on the calculated quantities

### Food Preparation
- Choose recipes that can be prepared in advance, such as stews, rice dishes, and desserts
- Break down the tasks and assign them to different team members
- Follow food safety guidelines:

| Safety Rule                                     | Reason                                           |
|-------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Wash hands thoroughly before handling food      | To prevent the spread of bacteria and illness    |
| Use separate cutting boards for raw and cooked food | To avoid cross-contamination                  |
| Store food at the correct temperature           | To prevent bacterial growth and spoilage         |
| Cook food to the appropriate internal temperature | To kill harmful bacteria and ensure food safety |

- Prepare dishes according to the recipes, ensuring consistency in taste and appearance
- Use appropriate equipment for large-scale cooking, such as large pots, pans, and serving dishes [IMAGE: Large cooking pots and utensils set up for catering event]

### Serving Food
- Set up an attractive buffet table with tablecloths, decorations, and labels for each dish
- Arrange dishes in a logical order, with starters and main courses separated
- Use chafing dishes or warming trays to keep food at a safe temperature during serving [IMAGE: Attractively arranged buffet table with chafing dishes]
- Provide serving utensils for each dish to avoid cross-contamination
- Have team members available to replenish dishes and assist guests as needed

## Practical Activity
Plan and execute a small-scale catering event for a school function or classroom celebration.

Materials:
- Ingredients for chosen recipes (locally available in Ghana)
- Cooking equipment (pots, pans, utensils)
- Serving dishes, utensils, and tablecloths
- Decorations for the buffet table

Instructions:
1. Divide the class into teams of 4-5 students
2. Each team should plan a menu for the event, including a starter, main course, and dessert
3. Calculate the quantities of ingredients needed based on the number of guests
4. Assign tasks to each team member (e.g., cooking, setting up the buffet, serving)
5. Prepare the dishes according to the recipes, following food safety guidelines
6. Set up an attractive buffet table with the prepared dishes, labels, and decorations
7. Serve the food to the guests, ensuring that the buffet is replenished as needed
8. Clean up after the event, washing dishes and storing leftover food properly

Expected Outcomes:
- Students will work collaboratively to plan and execute a successful catering event
- Students will apply skills in menu planning, quantity calculation, food preparation, and serving
- Students will gain practical experience in catering that can be applied to future career opportunities

## Career Connections
Catering skills are valuable in various careers in Ghana, such as:
- Caterer or chef in a hotel, restaurant, or event venue
- Entrepreneur starting a catering business or food delivery service
- Event planner organizing weddings, conferences, or corporate events
- Hospitality manager overseeing food service operations in a hotel or resort

## Key Vocabulary

| Term                 | Definition                                                                      |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Catering             | The business of providing food and drink for events                            |
| Menu planning        | The process of creating a list of dishes to be served at an event               |
| Quantity guide       | A tool used to calculate the amount of ingredients needed based on the number of people |
| Cross-contamination  | The transfer of bacteria from one food item to another, often through improper handling |
| Chafing dish         | A metal dish with a heating source used to keep food warm during serving        |
| Buffet               | A style of serving food where dishes are arranged on a table for guests to serve themselves |
| Food safety          | The practice of handling, preparing, and storing food in a way that prevents foodborne illness |
| Dietary restriction  | A limitation on the types of food a person can eat due to health, religious, or personal reasons |
| Consistency          | The degree to which food items are prepared and presented in the same way each time |
| Replenish            | To fill up or replace a supply of something, such as food on a buffet table     |
