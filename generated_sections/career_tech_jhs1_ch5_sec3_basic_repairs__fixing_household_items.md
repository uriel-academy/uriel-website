### Common Household Items That May Need Repairs
Many items in our homes can benefit from basic repairs, including:
- Clothing and textiles: holes, tears, loose buttons
- Furniture: loose legs, broken parts, scratches
- Kitchenware: loose handles, small cracks or chips
- Leather goods: tears, holes, worn areas

### Basic Repair Techniques
1. Mending holes or tears in fabric:
   - Choose a thread color that matches the fabric.
   - Thread the needle and tie a knot at the end.
   - Sew small stitches back and forth across the hole or tear, pulling the edges together.
   - Tie off the thread securely when finished.

2. Reattaching loose buttons:
   - Thread the needle and tie a knot at the end.
   - Position the button on the fabric and sew through the holes several times.
   - On the backside of the fabric, tie off the thread securely.

3. Fixing loose furniture parts:
   - Apply wood glue to the loose part and its attachment point.
   - Press the pieces together firmly and hold in place.
   - Allow the glue to dry completely according to the manufacturer's instructions.

4. Repairing small cracks or chips in kitchenware:
   - Clean the damaged area thoroughly.
   - Apply a suitable adhesive (such as epoxy) to the crack or chip.
   - Press the pieces together and wipe away any excess adhesive.
   - Allow the adhesive to dry completely before using the item.

## Practical Activity
1. Mending a torn piece of kente cloth:
   - Materials: torn kente cloth, needle, thread (matching color), scissors
   - Instructions:
     1. Cut a small piece of kente cloth to use as a patch.
     2. Place the patch over the tear on the backside of the fabric.
     3. Thread the needle and tie a knot at the end.
     4. Sew small stitches around the edges of the patch to secure it in place.
     5. Tie off the thread securely when finished.
   - Expected outcome: The torn kente cloth should have a neatly sewn patch covering the hole, making it usable again.

2. Repairing a loose handle on a cooking pot:
   - Materials: cooking pot with loose handle, pliers, screwdriver
   - Instructions:
     1. Examine the handle attachment to determine if any screws or rivets are loose.
     2. Use the screwdriver to tighten any loose screws.
     3. If the handle is still loose, use pliers to gently bend the metal back into place.
     4. Test the handle to ensure it is securely attached.
   - Expected outcome: The cooking pot should have a firmly attached handle, making it safe to use again.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Repair | To fix or mend something that is broken or damaged |
| Mend | To repair a hole or tear in fabric by sewing |
| Patch | A piece of material used to cover a hole or damaged area |
| Adhesive | A substance used to stick things together, such as glue |
| Rivet | A metal fastener used to join two pieces of material together |
| Sew | To join or repair fabric using a needle and thread |
| Stitch | A single pass of the needle and thread through the fabric |
| Tighten | To make something more secure or firm |
| Alteration | A change made to an item of clothing to improve its fit or appearance |
| Maintenance | Regular care and upkeep to keep something in good condition |
