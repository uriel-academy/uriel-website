## Main Content
### The Immune System
The immune system is our body's natural defense against infectious agents, such as bacteria, viruses, and parasites. It consists of various organs, cells, and proteins that work together to identify and eliminate foreign invaders. The main components of the immune system include:

- **White blood cells**: These cells are responsible for identifying and destroying pathogens. There are two main types of white blood cells: **phagocytes**, which engulf and digest foreign particles, and **lymphocytes**, which produce antibodies and coordinate the immune response.

- **Lymphoid organs**: These include the **spleen**, **thymus**, and **lymph nodes**. They produce and store lymphocytes, filter pathogens from the blood and lymph, and activate the immune response when needed.

- **Antibodies**: These are specialized proteins produced by **B lymphocytes** that recognize and bind to specific antigens on the surface of pathogens, marking them for destruction.

### Common Infectious Diseases in Ghana
Ghana, like many tropical countries, is prone to various infectious diseases. Some of the most common ones include:

1. **Malaria**: Caused by the *Plasmodium* parasite and transmitted by the *Anopheles* mosquito. Symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and fatigue.

2. **Cholera**: A water-borne disease caused by the bacterium *Vibrio cholerae*. It causes severe diarrhea and dehydration, which can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

3. **Typhoid fever**: Caused by the bacterium *Salmonella typhi* and spread through contaminated food or water. Symptoms include high fever, abdominal pain, and weakness.

4. **Schistosomiasis**: Also known as bilharzia, this parasitic disease is caused by flatworms and is contracted through contact with contaminated water. It can lead to chronic health problems if left untreated.

| Disease | Causative Agent | Mode of Transmission |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| Malaria | *Plasmodium* parasite | *Anopheles* mosquito |
| Cholera | *Vibrio cholerae* bacterium | Contaminated water |
| Typhoid fever | *Salmonella typhi* bacterium | Contaminated food or water |
| Schistosomiasis | Flatworms | Contact with contaminated water |

### Sanitation and Waste Disposal
Proper sanitation and waste disposal are essential for maintaining public health and preventing the spread of infectious diseases. Some key practices include:

- Washing hands regularly with soap and clean water, especially before eating and after using the toilet.
- Ensuring proper disposal of human waste through the use of toilets or latrines.
- Keeping the environment clean by properly disposing of solid waste and avoiding open defecation.
- Treating drinking water through boiling, filtering, or chemical disinfection to eliminate pathogens.

Practical example: Students can create a simple handwashing station using a jerry can with a tap, soap, and a basin for wastewater. This can be placed near the toilet or eating area to encourage regular handwashing.

### Personal Hygiene Plan
To maintain good health and prevent the spread of diseases, students should develop and follow a personal hygiene plan. This plan should include:

1. Daily bathing with soap and clean water.
2. Brushing teeth at least twice a day.
3. Wearing clean clothes and undergarments.
4. Keeping nails short and clean.
5. Covering the mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.
6. Avoiding close contact with people who are sick.
7. Seeking medical attention when experiencing symptoms of infectious diseases.

## Worked Examples
1. Calculating the concentration of a disinfectant solution:
   - To make a 0.5% sodium hypochlorite solution, you need to dilute a 5% stock solution.
   - Using the formula: C₁V₁ = C₂V₂, where C₁ is the concentration of the stock solution, V₁ is the volume of the stock solution needed, C₂ is the desired concentration, and V₂ is the final volume of the solution.
   - Let's say we want to make 1 liter (1000 mL) of 0.5% solution.
   - C₁ = 5%, V₁ = unknown, C₂ = 0.5%, V₂ = 1000 mL
   - (5%)(V₁) = (0.5%)(1000 mL)
   - V₁ = (0.5%)(1000 mL) ÷ 5% = 100 mL
   - Therefore, to make 1 liter of 0.5% sodium hypochlorite solution, you need to dilute 100 mL of the 5% stock solution with 900 mL of water.

2. Calculating the number of people infected with a disease:
   - In a village with a population of 1000 people, 5% of the population is infected with malaria.
   - To calculate the number of people infected, multiply the population by the percentage infected.
   - Number of people infected = 1000 × 0.05 = 50
   - Therefore, 50 people in the village are infected with malaria.
