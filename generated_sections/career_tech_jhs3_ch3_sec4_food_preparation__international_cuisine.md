## Main Content
### Common International Cuisines
Some popular international cuisines include:
- Chinese cuisine: known for stir-fries, rice dishes, and use of soy sauce and ginger
- Italian cuisine: famous for pasta dishes, pizza, and use of tomatoes, olive oil, and cheese
- Mexican cuisine: features tacos, burritos, and use of chili peppers, corn, and beans

### Comparing International Cuisines to Ghanaian Cuisine
While international cuisines may use different ingredients and cooking methods, they often share some similarities with Ghanaian cuisine:

| Cuisine | Similarities to Ghanaian Cuisine | Differences from Ghanaian Cuisine |
|---------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Chinese | Use of rice, stews, and soups    | Use of soy sauce, stir-frying     |
| Italian | Use of tomatoes, starchy foods   | Use of pasta, cheese              |  
| Mexican | Use of beans, spicy flavors      | Use of corn tortillas, chili peppers |

### Preparing an International Dish with Local Ingredients
[IMAGE: ingredients for a simple stir-fry: chicken, vegetables, oil, soy sauce, ginger, garlic]

To make a simple chicken stir-fry:
1. Cut chicken into small pieces and season with salt, pepper, and ginger
2. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over high heat
3. Add chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes
4. Add chopped vegetables (e.g. cabbage, carrots, onions) and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes
5. Add a small amount of soy sauce and stir until vegetables are tender
6. Serve hot with boiled rice

### Food Safety and Hygiene
When preparing any dish, it's important to follow proper food safety and hygiene practices:

[IMAGE: steps for handwashing, food storage, and kitchen cleanliness]

- Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water before handling food
- Keep raw meats separate from other ingredients to avoid cross-contamination 
- Cook foods to safe internal temperatures (e.g. 74°C for poultry)
- Store leftovers in covered containers in the refrigerator and reheat thoroughly before consuming

## Practical Activity
Try preparing a simple Mexican-inspired bean and rice dish using local ingredients:

Materials needed:
- 1 cup of rice
- 1 can of black-eyed peas or other local beans
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 tomato, chopped
- Salt, pepper, and chili powder to taste
- Oil for cooking

Instructions:
1. Cook rice according to package instructions
2. In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat
3. Add onion and garlic, and sauté until soft
4. Add beans, tomato, salt, pepper, and chili powder 
5. Simmer until heated through, about 5-7 minutes
6. Serve bean mixture over rice

Expected outcome: A flavorful rice and bean dish with Mexican-inspired seasonings

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Cuisine | A style of cooking characterized by distinctive ingredients, techniques, and dishes |
| Stir-fry | A Chinese cooking technique involving frying ingredients quickly over high heat |
| Pasta | An Italian food made from wheat flour, water, and sometimes egg |
| Tortilla | A thin, flat bread made from corn or wheat, used in Mexican cuisine |
| Soy sauce | A Chinese condiment made from fermented soybeans, used to add salty, savory flavor |
| Chili powder | A spice made from ground dried chili peppers, used in Mexican cuisine |
| Cross-contamination | The transfer of harmful bacteria from one food (usually raw) to another |
| Internal temperature | The temperature inside a food, used to determine if it has been cooked to a safe level |
| Sauté | To cook food quickly in a small amount of oil over relatively high heat |
| Simmer | To cook food gently in liquid at a temperature just below the boiling point |
