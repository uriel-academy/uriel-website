## Main Content
### What is a Budget?
A **budget** is a plan that helps you manage your money by tracking your **income** (the money you earn) and your **expenses** (the money you spend). It's a tool that allows you to see where your money is going and make informed decisions about how to allocate your resources.

### Key Components of a Budget
A basic household budget includes the following components:
1. **Income**: This is the money you earn from various sources, such as a job, business, or allowance.
2. **Fixed Expenses**: These are expenses that stay the same every month, such as rent, school fees, or transportation costs.
3. **Variable Expenses**: These are expenses that can change from month to month, such as food, clothing, or entertainment.
4. **Savings**: This is the money you set aside for future goals, emergencies, or investments.

### Creating a Simple Budget
To create a simple budget, follow these steps:
1. List your monthly income from all sources.
2. List your fixed expenses.
3. List your variable expenses.
4. Subtract your total expenses (fixed + variable) from your total income.
5. If there's money left over, allocate some to savings and decide how to spend the rest. If your expenses are more than your income, look for ways to cut back on variable expenses or increase your income.

### Making Informed Decisions
Once you have a budget, you can use it to make informed decisions about your spending and saving. For example, if you're a tailor and you want to invest in a new sewing machine, your budget can help you determine how much you can afford to save each month towards that goal. Or, if you're a food seller and you notice that you're spending too much on ingredients, you can look for ways to cut costs without sacrificing quality.

## Practical Activity
1. Create a personal budget for the next month using the template provided.
   - Materials needed: pencil, paper, calculator (optional)
   - List your expected income from all sources (allowance, gifts, etc.)
   - List your anticipated expenses (school supplies, food, transportation, etc.)
   - Subtract your total expenses from your total income
   - Allocate any remaining money to savings or other goals
   - Share your budget with a classmate and discuss ways to improve it
2. As a class, create a budget for a hypothetical small business (e.g., a waakye seller or a batik artist).
   - Brainstorm potential income sources and expenses
   - Use a spreadsheet or table to organize the information
   - Discuss ways to maximize profits and minimize costs
   - Present your budget to the class and explain your decision-making process
